Quantum computing workloads use a mix of classical processors and quantum processors. The “processor” needed depends on the task being performed.

1. Quantum Processing Units (QPUs)

These are the actual quantum chips that run quantum algorithms.

Major QPU Types

TechnologyUsed ByKey Feature

2. Classical CPUs Needed Alongside Quantum Computers

Quantum computers do not replace normal processors. They require powerful classical systems to:

  • Control qubits
  • Run error correction
  • Compile quantum circuits
  • Pre/post-process results
  • Simulate quantum systems

Common CPU Types

  • x86 servers:

* Intel Xeon

* Advanced Micro Devices EPYC

  • ARM processors:

* NVIDIA Grace

* Ampere Computing

Quantum systems today are actually hybrid systems:

Classical CPU + GPU + Quantum Processor

3. GPUs for Quantum Computing

GPUs are heavily used for:

  • Quantum simulation
  • AI-assisted quantum research
  • Tensor calculations
  • Error correction modeling

Popular GPUs

  • NVIDIA H100 / B200
  • Advanced Micro Devices Instinct MI300

4. Specialized Quantum Control Processors

These are custom chips designed specifically to manage qubits.

Examples

  • Intel Horse Ridge
  • IBM Quantum control electronics
  • NVIDIA DGX Quantum platform

These processors handle:

  • Microwave pulse generation
  • Real-time qubit feedback
  • Error mitigation
  • Cryogenic control systems

5. What Processor Is Needed for Different Quantum Tasks?

TaskMain Processor Needed

6. Important Reality About Quantum Computing

Most current “quantum computing” work today is actually:

  • simulation,
  • orchestration,
  • compiler optimization,
  • AI modeling,
  • or cloud-based access to QPUs.

That means high-end classical hardware is still essential.
