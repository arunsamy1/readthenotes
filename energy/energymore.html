There isn't a single, definitive number for new data centers completed in the U.S. this year because the buildout is happening at two different levels: the number of physical facilities and their total power capacity.

However, the most accurate answer is that while construction has started on many projects, very few new data centers are expected to be fully completed in 2026 due to widespread delays . Think of it as a massive construction zone where ground has been broken everywhere, but only a small fraction will have the final "ribbon cutting" this year.

⚡ Why "Capacity" Matters More Than "Count"

When tracking the AI boom, investors and analysts focus on Megawatts (MW) or Gigawatts (GW) of power capacity, not just the number of buildings. A single modern AI data center can require as much electricity as a nuclear power plant (1 GW), whereas older facilities used far less .

🚧 The Biggest Hurdles to Completion

The main reason the "finished" number is so low is that builders face three major obstacles:

  • The Power Grid is Too Slow: Building a data center takes about 18-36 months, but building the transmission lines and power plants to run it takes 5 to 10 years .
  • Parts are Missing: Key electrical components like large transformers now have wait times of up to 128 weeks (over 2 years) because U.S. manufacturing can't keep up .
  • Labor Shortages: There simply aren't enough specialized workers (electricians, plumbers) to finish all these massive buildings at the same time .

🔮 Looking Ahead to 2027 and Beyond

Even though 2026 is shaping up to be a year of delays, the long-term outlook is enormous.

  • Next Year's Pipeline: Over 25 GW of capacity is planned for 2027, though less than 10 GW is currently under construction .
  • The Long Game: The tech giants are not slowing down. Amazon, Google, Meta, and Microsoft are projected to spend $600 billion to $700 billion on AI infrastructure (mostly data centers) in 2026 alone .
