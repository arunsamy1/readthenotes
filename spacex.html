🔍 How to Identify More Companies

Finding a complete list is difficult because many relationships are not public. If you want to find more on your own, here are the best strategies:

  • Focus on ETFs: The TEMA Space Innovators ETF (ticker: NASA) explicitly tracks the space economy. A large portion of its holdings are in publicly-traded companies that are direct or indirect partners of SpaceX, making its holdings a great starting point for research .
  • Look for Prime Contractors: Follow major defense and space prime contractors like Northrop Grumman ($NOC) and Lockheed Martin ($LMT). While they are often seen as competitors, they also purchase engines, components, and launch services from SpaceX for their own projects .
  • Monitor SEC Filings: Pay attention to material contracts and customer concentrations in the 10-K and 10-Q filings of small and mid-cap space companies. A mention of a significant launch services agreement or a long-term supply deal for a major satellite constellation is a strong signal.
